# Independent product-management adversarial review

Perspective: principal product manager responsible for a credible wedge, adoption, trust, and release learning.

## Verdict

“Incogni but free” is not a defensible or honest wedge. MyCogni should win on local custody, proof, minimum disclosure, and inspectable connector quality for a technically comfortable U.S. self-hoster. The existing plan is too broad for first value and lacks explicit PMF gates.

## User evidence themes

People value time saved, recurrence, simple setup, aliases/historical attributes, custom-case coverage, specific must-have brokers, visible dates, and a credible path when the broker resists. They dislike subscription dependence, hidden manual work, unexplained stalls, opaque recheck cadence, headline coverage counts, and especially “completed” without proof.

The strongest independent warning is empirical: the Consumer Reports/Tall Poppy study found 35% of observed profiles removed across evaluated services after four months, and manual opt-outs reached 70% in its small sample. The PETS study found low overlap, 41.1% average user-confirmed record-link accuracy, and 48.2% average removal effectiveness for identified records across measured services. Product truth must preserve denominators and uncertainty.

Anonymous reviews are hypotheses. The supplied Reddit thread includes both detailed useful complaints and promotional/synthetic-review concerns. No roadmap priority should rely on sentiment volume from it.

## V1 recommendation

Support one consenting adult per installation while retaining an isolated multi-profile domain model. Deliver current/historical aliases; a separately authorized read-only exposure preview over a small public set; attribute-level match explanations; guided/manual flows; 2–5 trusted automatic capabilities; exact disclosure preview/ledger; reason/owner/next-date on every case; independently corroborated outcomes; resurfacing; kill switches; backup/export/delete; and a generated capability support matrix.

Custom URL intake may produce a safe guided draft in v1. It may not automatically remove arbitrary content. Family/guardian administration, hundreds of heuristic connectors, blanket private-broker outreach, hosted SaaS, non-U.S. claims, mobile, insurance/breach bundles, and model dependency are deferred.

## PMF thesis

Initial customer: U.S.-based developer, security/privacy practitioner, homelabber, or technically comfortable self-hoster who distrusts centralized removal services and values evidence more than a huge count.

Job: “Show where my high-risk people-search data is exposed, remove it with the least disclosure possible, prove what changed, and keep checking without becoming another company holding my dossier.”

Do not initially target people in acute stalking/activist/journalist danger as the primary segment until rapid response and threat-informed human escalation exist; failure cost is too high for a volunteer alpha.

## Required experiments

1. **Exposure-preview pilot (10–15 people):** preregistered setup, precision and usefulness denominators; zero removal submissions. Real scans still require separate consent and disclosure.
2. **Proof comprehension:** no participant mistakes acknowledgement for verified deletion; at least 80% state the next action unaided.
3. **Switching interviews:** at least five users each of commercial/DIY paths; test whether custody, proof, broker specificity, recurrence, or price drives switching; hypothesis: 40% of target users will install Docker.
4. **Twelve-week beta:** track day-30/day-90 scheduler retention, manual minutes, verified outcome rate, resurfacing response, connector quarantine, and fields disclosed; hypotheses: under 10 manual minutes/month and at least 60% day-90 scheduler retention.
5. **Disclosure comprehension:** every automatic-action pilot user identifies destination and field categories during setup authorization.
6. **Contributor funnel:** a first contribution runs synthetic connector tests within 30 minutes and exposes provenance/maturity automatically.
7. **Post-v1 local-assist shadow test:** proceed only after at least 30% task-time reduction with no semantic, disclosure, safety, or false-positive regression.
